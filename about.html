How We Make It

The Science of Electrolytic Copper Powder

1
Electrolytic Deposition

Copper ions from copper sulfate-sulfuric acid electrolyte are reduced at the cathode under precisely controlled conditions. Current density (150–300 A/m²), copper concentration (5–10 g/L), acid (120–150 g/L), and temperature (45–55°C) are continuously monitored to achieve dendritic morphology.

2
Washing & Filtration

Multi-stage washing with deionized water removes electrolyte residues. Target: <0.01% residual electrolyte. Filtration separates powder from wash water. Quality check at this stage ensures purity specifications are met.

3
Reduction & Drying

Hydrogen or reducing atmosphere treatment (300–400°C) eliminates surface oxides formed during washing. Controlled drying reduces moisture to <0.2%. This step is critical for achieving final purity >99.80% Cu.

4
Milling & Classification

Gentle de-agglomeration breaks up powder clusters without destroying dendritic morphology. Precision screening per ASTM B214 classifies powder into specific mesh size ranges. Final blending ensures homogeneous batches.

Chemical Reactions: Anode: Cu → Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻  |  Cathode: Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Cu (powder) — The electrochemical deposition process is what creates the unique dendritic morphology that makes our powder superior for critical applications.
